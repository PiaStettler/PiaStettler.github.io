As long as I can think of I was fascinated by nature and enjoyed observing and looking for patterns.
I sorted and counted everything I found. From clam and snail shells over Lego bricks to books.
Thus, once I learned about statistics and the methods for describing patterns and testing hypotheses I was hooked.

In my master thesis I studied the effect of serotonin on social behaviour in a social fish species.
I found that serotonin is involved in the modulation of sociopositive and aggressive behaviour and that the effect of the neurotransmitter seems to be context-dependent.
If you want to know more details, check out the papers we published:

  • Hormones and Behaviour
  • Behavioural Brain Research
